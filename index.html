Continuous Target Speech Extraction: Enhancing Personalized Diarization and Extraction on Long Recordings

Submitted to ICASSP 2024

Authors: He Zhao & Hangting Chen & Jianwei Yu & Chao Weng

School of Information and Electronic Engineering, Zhejiang University & Tencent AI Lab, Audio and Speech Signal Processing Oteam

Email: zhao_he@zju.edu.cn

Abstract: Target speaker extraction (TSE) can extract the target speaker's voice from the input mixture sound. Previous works mainly focus on high-overlapping scenarios. For example, the DNS challenge evaluates the TSE on single-speaker noisy and highly overlapped multi-speaker noisy conditions. However, real-world applications usually meet more complex scenarios like variable speaker overlapping and speaker absence. In this study, we propose a framework to perform continuous TSE (C-TSE), which consists of a target speaker voice activation detection (TSVAD) and a TSE model. This framework alleviates the issue of the poor performance of TSE on similar speakers and the lack of personal enhancement ability of diarization. In detail, unlike conventional TSVAD deployed to refine the diarization results, the proposed TSVAD uses transformer blocks to generate the timestamps of the target speaker straightforwardly, independent of the diarization methods. Moreover, we explore the different integration methods of TSVAD and TSE by comparing the parallel and cascaded methods. The framework is evaluated on various metrics, including diarization and enhancement metrics. Experiment results illustrate that the TSVAD exhibits lower diarization errors when compared with classic methods. Sequential cascaded fusion of TSVAD and TSE further improves the extraction performance.

A Quick Review

Background

Previous target speaker extraction mainly focused on high-overlapping scenarios. For example, the DNS challenge evaluates the TSE on single-speaker noisy and highly overlapped multi-speaker noisy conditions. However, real-world applications usually meet more complex scenarios like variable speaker overlapping and speaker absence.

Experimental setup

The experiments were performed on a simulated dataset. The test set is divided into four configurations based on the number of speakers: 2, 3, 4, 5. Each configuration corresponds to six overlap rates.

Framework & Method

(1) The introduction of transformer blocks improves the performance of TSVAD.

(2) For the continuous target speech extraction task in complex scenes, we propose the C-TSE network.

Results

(1) TSVAD model is introduced with transformer blocks to capture the target speaker's activity, surpassing the classic clustering-based methods.

(2) Joint framework for C-TSE is proposed by the parallel fusion of TSVAD and TSE results, further promoting the performance on diarization and enhancement metrics.
